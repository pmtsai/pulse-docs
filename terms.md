## 1. What Pulse is

Pulse is a personal AI health assistant that combines wearable data, calendar, email, and clinical records into daily briefs, trend analysis, and proactive recommendations. Pulse is operated as a personal tool by the owner identified below.

## 2. Who can use Pulse

Pulse is intended for adult individual use on the user's own data. Pulse is not a replacement for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always seek the advice of a qualified clinician for any questions regarding a medical condition. Never disregard professional medical advice or delay seeking it because of content in Pulse.

## 3. Data you authorize Pulse to access

By connecting an integration (including Epic FHIR / MyChart, Oura, Apple Health, Google Calendar, Gmail), you authorize Pulse to read data from that source on your behalf for the duration of the connection. You may revoke any connection at any time from Settings → Integrations. Revoking a connection stops future sync immediately.

Read-only for all clinical integrations. Pulse never writes back to Epic or any other EHR.

## 4. How Pulse handles your data

See the [Disclosure & Data Handling page](disclosure.md) for full detail. Summary:

- Data is encrypted at rest and in transit
- Scoped per user; never shared between users
- Never used to train external AI / LLM models
- Never sold, shared with advertisers, insurers, employers, or data brokers
- Third-party LLM processing (Anthropic) is configured with no-training settings and retains no data beyond a single request

## 5. Your rights

- **Export:** request a full export of your data at any time, in JSON or human-readable format
- **Delete:** delete individual records or your entire account from Settings. Account deletion purges all associated records within 30 days.
- **Disconnect:** revoke any integration at any time to stop future data sync
